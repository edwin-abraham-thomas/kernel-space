# Creator Voice Profile

Use this as the spoken-style reference for kernel_space scripts when the goal is to sound like Edwin talking, not like a polished article being read aloud.

## Core Voice

- Sounds like a working engineer talking to another engineer.
- Conversational Manglish in English alphabets.
- Calm, direct, and practical.
- Curious and thoughtful, but not dramatic.
- Confident without sounding salesy or performative.
- Mildly opinionated when needed, but not aggressive.

## Audience Relationship

- Speak to working engineers first.
- Aspiring engineers should feel invited, but never talked down to.
- Assume the listener is smart and busy.
- Respect the listener by getting to the point quickly.

## Tone Rules

- Prefer natural spoken phrasing over written-looking phrasing.
- Prefer "this is what I focus on" over "this is what interests me" if the latter sounds translated.
- Prefer personal judgment language like "enikku thonnunnathu" when making a claim that is interpretive.
- Prefer grounded statements over slogans.
- Prefer practitioner language over content-creator language.
- Prefer understated confidence over hype.

## Language Habits

- Use short to medium spoken sentences.
- It is fine to use a few longer flowing sentences when the rhythm feels natural.
- Mix English tech terms naturally into Malayalam sentences.
- Use plain everyday Manglish, not formal Malayalam.
- Do not sound like English was written first and then translated.
- Avoid too many stacked sentence fragments unless they are needed for emphasis.
- Avoid repeating the same connector too often.
- Keep the words easy to hear and understand on first listen.

## Preferred Phrasing Patterns

- "Njan focus cheyyunna karyangal ennu paranjal..."
- "Enikku thonnunnathu..."
- "Actually ivideyokke aane..."
- "... ennathaanu"
- "... ennanu njan nokkiyath"
- "... pattumo ennathaanu"
- "... aane"
- "... budhimuttane"
- "Appo..."
- "Pakshe..."

These are not mandatory phrases. They show the general speaking rhythm: direct, personal, and slightly reflective.

## Word Choice Preferences

Prefer:

- "focus cheyyunna karyangal"
- "work cheyyunna karyangal" / "work cheyyatha karyangal"
- "idapedendi vannath"
- "personal engineering workspace"
- "karyamulla content" or "detailed aaya content" when needed
- "Malayala-thil" when it feels natural in spoken context
- "AI use cheythulla workflows"

Avoid or use carefully:

- "genuinely"
- "experimentation vehicle"
- "proof point"
- "signal-heavy"
- "fluff kurachu"
- "time fill cheyyan"
- "vibe coding" unless the contrast is useful and intentional
- overly formal words like "inevitable failures" if a simpler spoken version works
- hard corporate phrasing or marketing words

## Structural Habits

- Open with a practical tension or observation.
- Move quickly to what the real issue is.
- Explain with concrete spoken examples rather than abstract labels.
- When listing things, prefer a flowing spoken list over a bullet-sounding recital.
- End sections with a line that sounds like a human thought, not a headline.
- Close with a short, clean CTA. Do not over-explain the CTA.

## What This Voice Sounds Like

- "AI code generate cheyyum. Pakshe engane build cheyyanam, evide issue varam, naale complexity koodumbolum ithu nannayi nilkkumo... athokke AI thanne decide cheyyunnathe budhimuttane."
- "Njan focus cheyyunna karyangal ennu paranjal, AI use cheythu software build cheyyunnathu mathram alla..."
- "Actually ivideyokke aane engineers-inde value kooduthal visible aakunnathe."
- "TinqrBox-il njan kooduthal focus cheythath, AI use cheythu oru app-inulla complete shell build cheyyan pattumo ennathaanu."

## What This Voice Does Not Sound Like

- Overly polished lecture language.
- Heavy motivational tone.
- Creator-influencer energy.
- Corporate presentation phrasing.
- Literary Malayalam.
- Overloaded jargon without explanation.
- Too many dramatic pauses or grand claims.

## Script Writing Checks

Before finalizing a script, check:

- Does this sound like something I would naturally say out loud?
- Does any line feel translated from English rather than spoken in Manglish?
- Is any phrase too corporate, too formal, or too dramatic?
- Are the tradeoffs and realities clear enough for a working engineer?
- Is the CTA short and natural?

## Use This Profile When

- drafting new `script.md` files
- rewriting stiff sections into more natural speech
- checking whether a line feels too formal, too robotic, or too content-creatory
- keeping tone consistent across videos
